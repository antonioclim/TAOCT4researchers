# Unit 8: Further Reading and Resources

## Primary Textbooks

### Cormen, T. H., Leiserson, C. E., Rivest, R. L., & Stein, C. (2009)
*Introduction to Algorithms* (3rd ed.). MIT Press.

The definitive algorithms textbook. Chapters 15-16 provide rigorous treatment of dynamic programming with detailed proofs and extensive examples.

**Key Chapters:**
- Chapter 4: Divide-and-Conquer
- Chapter 15: Dynamic Programming
- Chapter 16: Greedy Algorithms

---

### Sedgewick, R., & Wayne, K. (2011)
*Algorithms* (4th ed.). Addison-Wesley.

Accessible treatment with excellent visualisations and Java implementations. Companion website offers interactive demos.

**Key Sections:**
- 2.2: Mergesort
- 2.3: Quicksort
- Section on Dynamic Programming

**Online Resources:** [algs4.cs.princeton.edu](https://algs4.cs.princeton.edu/)

---

## Supplementary Books

### Skiena, S. S. (2020)
*The Algorithm Design Manual* (3rd ed.). Springer.

Practical guide with "war stories" from real applications. Excellent for bridging theory and practice.

### Dasgupta, S., Papadimitriou, C. H., & Vazirani, U. V. (2008)
*Algorithms*. McGraw-Hill.

Concise and lucid treatment. Free draft available online.

### Bellman, R. (1957)
*Dynamic Programming*. Princeton University Press.

The original treatise by the founder of dynamic programming. Historical importance.

## Research Papers

### Bellman, R. (1952)
"On the Theory of Dynamic Programming." *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences*, 38(8), 716-719.

The foundational paper introducing dynamic programming.

### Michie, D. (1968)
"Memo Functions and Machine Learning." *Nature*, 218, 19-22.

Introduces the term "memoisation" and its applications.

### Needleman, S. B., & Wunsch, C. D. (1970)
"A General Method Applicable to the Search for Similarities in the Amino Acid Sequence of Two Proteins." *Journal of Molecular Biology*, 48(3), 443-453.

DP applied to bioinformatics sequence alignment.
