## üìö Textbooks and Reference Books

### 1. Introduction to Algorithms (CLRS)
**Cormen, T. H., Leiserson, C. E., Rivest, R. L., & Stein, C. (2022).** *Introduction to Algorithms* (4th ed.). MIT Press.

The definitive reference for algorithm design and analysis. Chapters 10-12 cover fundamental data structures, Chapter 22 covers graph algorithms and Chapters 24-25 cover shortest paths.

**Relevance**: Comprehensive theoretical foundation for all Week 4 topics.

---

### 2. Algorithm Design Manual
**Skiena, S. S. (2020).** *The Algorithm Design Manual* (3rd ed.). Springer.

Practical approach to algorithm design with real-world applications. Includes a catalogue of algorithmic problems with solutions.

**Relevance**: Bridges theory and practice; excellent for research applications.

---

### 3. Algorithms
**Sedgewick, R., & Wayne, K. (2011).** *Algorithms* (4th ed.). Addison-Wesley.

Clear exposition of fundamental algorithms with Java implementations. Accompanying website with visualisations.

**Relevance**: Excellent visualisations of graph algorithms and data structures.

**URL**: https://algs4.cs.princeton.edu/

---

## üìÑ Seminal Papers

### 4. Bloom Filters
**Bloom, B. H. (1970).** Space/time trade-offs in hash coding with allowable errors. *Communications of the ACM*, 13(7), 422-426.

The original paper introducing Bloom filters. Remarkably readable and still highly relevant.

**DOI**: 10.1145/362686.362692

---

### 5. Count-Min Sketch
**Cormode, G., & Muthukrishnan, S. (2005).** An improved data stream summary: the count-min sketch and its applications. *Journal of Algorithms*, 55(1), 58-75.

Foundational paper for streaming algorithms. Introduces the Count-Min sketch with theoretical guarantees.

**DOI**: 10.1016/j.jalgor.2003.12.001

---

### 6. A* Search Algorithm
**Hart, P. E., Nilsson, N. J., & Raphael, B. (1968).** A formal basis for the heuristic determination of minimum cost paths. *IEEE Transactions on Systems Science and Cybernetics*, 4(2), 100-107.

The seminal paper introducing the A* algorithm with proofs of optimality and completeness.

**DOI**: 10.1109/TSSC.1968.300136

---

### 7. Cuckoo Hashing
**Pagh, R., & Rodler, F. F. (2004).** Cuckoo hashing. *Journal of Algorithms*, 51(2), 122-144.

Elegant hashing scheme with worst-case O(1) lookup. Foundational for modern hash table designs.

**DOI**: 10.1016/j.jalgor.2003.12.002

---
