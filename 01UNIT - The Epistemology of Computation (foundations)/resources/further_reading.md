## Essential Reading

### 1. Turing's Original Paper

**Turing, A. M. (1936).** "On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem." *Proceedings of the London Mathematical Society*, 42(2), 230-265.

*The foundational paper that started it all. Turing introduces his "a-machines" and proves the undecidability of the Halting Problem. Surprisingly readable for an 88-year-old mathematics paper.*

## Suggested Reading Order

For those new to the topic:

1. **Week 1-2:** Sipser Ch. 3-4, Petzold (The Annotated Turing)
2. **Week 3-4:** Hankin (Lambda Calculi), Pierce Ch. 5-6
3. **Week 5-6:** Abelson & Sussman Ch. 1-4
4. **Ongoing:** Nystrom (Crafting Interpreters)

For those with background:

1. **Immediate:** Turing's original paper, Hofstadter
2. **Deep dive:** Pierce (TAPL), Sipser Ch. 5-9
3. **Application:** Domain-specific text from your field
