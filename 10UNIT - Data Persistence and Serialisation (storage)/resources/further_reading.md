# Further Reading: Data Persistence and Serialisation

## Essential References

### Books

**Database Systems: The Complete Book** (2nd Edition)
Garcia-Molina, H., Ullman, J. D., & Widom, J. (2008)
Pearson Education.
*Comprehensive coverage of relational database theory and practice. Essential for understanding normalisation, query optimisation, and transaction management.*

**Python for Data Analysis** (3rd Edition)
McKinney, W. (2022)
O'Reilly Media.
*Practical guide to data manipulation in Python, including extensive coverage of pandas, file formats, and data cleaning.*

**The Hitchhiker's Guide to Python**
Reitz, K., & Schlusser, T. (2016)
O'Reilly Media.
*Established conventions for Python development, including file handling, packaging, and project structure.*

### Academic Papers

**Good Enough Practices in Scientific Computing**
Wilson, G., Bryan, J., Cranston, K., Kitzes, J., Nederbragt, L., & Teal, T. K. (2017)
*PLOS Computational Biology*, 13(6), e1005510.
https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pcbi.1005510
*Essential reading on practical approaches to reproducible research, including data management and file organisation.*

**Best Practices for Scientific Computing**
Wilson, G., et al. (2014)
*PLOS Biology*, 12(1), e1001745.
https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pbio.1001745
*Foundational principles for software development in research contexts.*

**Ten Simple Rules for Reproducible Computational Research**
Sandve, G. K., et al. (2013)
*PLOS Computational Biology*, 9(10), e1003285.
https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pcbi.1003285
*Practical guidelines for ensuring research reproducibility.*
