# 02UNIT Further Reading: Abstraction and Encapsulation

## üìö Essential Reading

### Books

1. **"Design Patterns: Elements of Reusable Object-Oriented Software"**
   - Authors: Gamma, Helm, Johnson, Vlissides (Gang of Four)
   - Publisher: Addison-Wesley, 1994
   - Relevance: The definitive catalogue of design patterns. Focus on Strategy (pp. 315-323), Observer (pp. 293-303) and Factory Method (pp. 107-116) for this week.
   - Level: Intermediate

2. **"Clean Code: A Handbook of Agile Software Craftsmanship"**
   - Author: Robert C. Martin
   - Publisher: Prentice Hall, 2008
   - Relevance: Chapters 10 (Classes) and 11 (Systems) directly relate to abstraction and encapsulation principles.
   - Level: Intermediate

3. **"Fluent Python"** (2nd Edition)
   - Author: Luciano Ramalho
   - Publisher: O'Reilly, 2022
   - Relevance: Chapters 11-13 cover protocols, ABCs and inheritance in depth with Pythonic idioms.
   - Level: Intermediate to Advanced

4. **"Architecture Patterns with Python"**
   - Authors: Harry Percival, Bob Gregory
   - Publisher: O'Reilly, 2020
   - Relevance: Excellent coverage of dependency injection and repository patterns for scientific applications.
   - Level: Intermediate
   - Free online: https://www.cosmicpython.com/

## üìÑ Academic Papers

5. **"On the Criteria To Be Used in Decomposing Systems into Modules"**
   - Author: David L. Parnas
   - Publication: Communications of the ACM, 1972
   - DOI: 10.1145/361598.361623
   - Relevance: Foundational paper on information hiding and modular design.

6. **"Abstraction Mechanisms in CLU"**
   - Authors: Barbara Liskov et al.
   - Publication: Communications of the ACM, 1977
   - Relevance: Origin of many OOP concepts including the Liskov Substitution Principle.

7. **"Design Patterns in Python for Machine Learning"**
   - Authors: Erich Gamma (updated perspectives)
   - Publication: Various conference talks, 2018-2020
   - Relevance: Modern application of patterns in data science contexts.
