## üìÑ Foundational Papers

### 5. Reproducibility Crisis in Science
**Title**: "Why Most Published Research Findings Are False"  
**Author**: John P. A. Ioannidis  
**Journal**: PLOS Medicine, 2005  
**DOI**: [10.1371/journal.pmed.0020124](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pmed.0020124)

The landmark paper that sparked widespread discussion about reproducibility in science. Essential context for understanding why reproducibility matters.

---

### 6. Good Enough Practices in Scientific Computing
**Title**: "Good Enough Practices in Scientific Computing"  
**Authors**: Greg Wilson et al.  
**Journal**: PLOS Computational Biology, 2017  
**DOI**: [10.1371/journal.pcbi.1005510](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pcbi.1005510)

Practical recommendations for computational research that balance rigour with real-world constraints. A must-read for researchers.

**Key recommendations**:
- Data management
- Software engineering
- Collaboration
- Project organisation

---

### 7. Ten Simple Rules for Reproducible Computational Research
**Title**: "Ten Simple Rules for Reproducible Computational Research"  
**Authors**: Geir Kjetil Sandve et al.  
**Journal**: PLOS Computational Biology, 2013  
**DOI**: [10.1371/journal.pcbi.1003285](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pcbi.1003285)

Concise, actionable guidelines for making computational research reproducible. Perfect for quick reference.
